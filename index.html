§ 1 · Introduction

Why Qubits Matter

Classical computers hit fundamental limits in chemistry, materials science, and optimisation. Quantum computing exploits superposition and entanglement to solve specific problem classes exponentially faster.

QUANTUM ADVANTAGE

Google's 53-qubit Sycamore (2019) performed a sampling task in 200 seconds that would take a classical supercomputer ~10,000 years.

THE SCALING PROBLEM

At 0.1% physical qubit error rate, ~1,000–10,000 physical qubits are needed to create one reliable logical qubit.

2.2 Entanglement & Bell States

Entanglement is a multi-particle state that cannot be factored into individual states. Measuring one qubit instantly determines the other — regardless of distance. The Bell states are the four maximally entangled two-qubit states.

2.3 Coherence Times — T₁ & T₂

Your quantum circuit must complete before the qubit decoheres. T₁ = energy relaxation (|1⟩→|0⟩). T₂ = phase coherence decay. Physical law: T₂ ≤ 2T₁.

3.1 Three Types of Physical Qubits

TYPE A · Intrinsic Two-Level

Nature provides exactly 2 states. No leakage possible.

Electron spin, photon polarisation, NMR nuclei

✓ Clean qubit space, no leakage errors

TYPE B · Two-Level Subset

Choose 2 states from a richer energy spectrum.

Trapped ions (hyperfine, Rydberg), NV centres

⚠ Auxiliary states aid gates but cause leakage

TYPE C · Engineered Two-Level

Fabricated Hilbert space — circuit design controls qubit properties.

Superconducting transmon (IBM, Google)

✓ Tunable / ✗ Fabrication variation

🎯

Universal Gate Set

ANY quantum algorithm decomposes into: (1) arbitrary single-qubit rotations + (2) one two-qubit entangling gate (typically CNOT). This is the quantum equivalent of NAND gates in classical computing.

4.3 Fault-Tolerant QC — The Destination

WHAT IS NEEDED
  • Logical qubit: encoded across many physical qubits
  • Surface code: 2D grid of physical qubits
  • At 0.1% error: ~1,000–10,000 physical per logical qubit
  • RSA-2048 needs ~4,000 logical qubits → millions of physical
KEY MILESTONES
  • Google (2023): Exponential error suppression in surface code
  • Harvard/QuEra (2023): 48 logical qubit operations
  • Threshold theorem: physical error <1% allows arbitrary suppression
  • IBM Heron (156 qubits) at AQV — India's largest QC
§ Conclusion

Key Takeaways

1

No Single Platform Has Won

Superconducting leads in scale, trapped ions in fidelity, neutral atoms in programmability. The race is ongoing.

2

Physical Error Rates Drive Everything

Reducing errors from 0.1% toward 0.01% shrinks logical qubit overhead by orders of magnitude.

3

NISQ Algorithms Are the Bridge

VQE and QAOA extract value from today's imperfect hardware through short-depth, classical-quantum hybrid loops.
